Introductory Session

This is the introductory session to a series of RaspberryPi workshops in collaboration with FabLab Seoul and innovation engineer Todd Holoubek.

On this event Todd will explain the basic concepts of what is an operating system, what is assembly code and other important basics. If you are interested in learning about RaspberryPi, you are welcome to visit this lecture for free. 

Bonus: you will get the chance to visit a real Fabrication Laboratory—a truly unique environment, and learn about technologies and benefits provided there.

Those who want to get their hands on RaspberryPi and make something real are welcome to participate in the subsequent series of workshops over the next four weeks.

RaspberryPi Workshop

This series of workshops is focused around the idea that we should not just make “things”, but that we should be innovating, experimenting, creating tools and systems that take us one step closer in understanding how our world works. By understanding how our world works, we are empowered to reshape our world!!

The outcome of these workshops will be an RFID logging and access system. Through designing and building this system, this series of workshops will teach:

• The basics of the Raspberry Pi platform

• Programming with the Python language

• Basic MySql database structure and commands

• Serial communication

• RFID technology

• Basic Electricity (with soldering)

The series of workshops is ₩40,000 which will be put towards food and beverages for each workshop! At the end of the workshops, each participant has the option to purchase their Raspberry Pi.!

About Todd Holoubek

Todd Holoubek, is raised on a small farm in Ixonia, Wisconsin. His teens to early twenties, are spent operating a small sports wear company and performing in the theatre. His undergraduate education is dedicated to the theater arts. He produces alive ensemble performance each semester for three years. Post-graduation the ensemble moves to television as “The State on MTV”, and runs through the mid 90’s.Post television, Todd attends graduate school at the Interactive Telecommunications Program, NYU. Graduating in 2001, he receives a residency at ITP. Over twenty years,in Manhattan, Todd grows into an artist and innovator of rich experiences. As a result,participants of his installations encounter media and technology through light sculptures, intriguing puzzles, and mischievous interactive works examining the bridge between humans, their technology, their media, and their cultures. In 2012, he moves to Korea.
