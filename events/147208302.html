We welcome a long-awaited practical workshop with Dr. Joshua James, which is our second event from the cyber security series. Bring your laptop to practice being a cyber-sleuth.

After a short discussion about the challenges with different data storage devices, we will learn to take a copy of Random Access Memory (USB stick for an advanced lab) and extract evidence of user activity, such as pictures, web addresses and etc.

Bonus

In addition, we will cover extracting passwords from memory.

============== What to bring ================

• Laptop


• USB stick or other storage device you want to recover data from (camera's SD card).


Windows Users:

• Download `FTK Imager Lite Version 3.1.1' [Free] (http://www.accessdata.com/support/product-downloads)


• Download `UnixUtils' [Free] (http://sourceforge.net/projects/unxutils/)


• Download `Testdisk & Photorec' [Free] (http://www.cgsecurity.org/wiki/TestDisk_Download)


Mac Users:

• Download `Mac Memory Reader' [Free] (http://cybermarshal.com/index.php/cyber-marshal-utilities/mac-memory-reader)

• Download `Testdisk & Photorec' [Free] (http://www.cgsecurity.org/wiki/TestDisk_Download)


Linux Users:

• Download `lime-forensics' [Free] (https://code.google.com/p/lime-forensics/)

• Download `Testdisk & Photorec' [Free] (http://www.cgsecurity.org/wiki/TestDisk_Download)


Android Users (advanced):

• Rooted Android device

• Download `lime-forensics' [Free] (https://code.google.com/p/lime-forensics/)


• A laptop to do acquisition analysis on with requirements above.
